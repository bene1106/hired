# Banned phrases for cover letter post-processing.
# One phrase per line. Comments start with #.
# Matching is case-insensitive, substring match.
#
# When to add to this list: a phrase that consistently makes generated text
# read as AI-slop or generic, AND that has a known better alternative.

# Generic enthusiasm
passionate about
excited to apply
thrilled to apply
delighted to apply
honored to apply

# Vague competencies
results-driven
results-oriented
team player
self-starter
go-getter
strong communicator
detail-oriented
hard-working
hard worker

# Cliché framing
synergies
synergy
think outside the box
outside the box
push the envelope
move the needle
hit the ground running

# Inflated claims (require specifics instead)
extensive experience
deep expertise
proven track record
demonstrated ability
exceptional skills
unparalleled
world-class

# Weasel openers
I am writing to apply for
I am writing to express my interest
Please find attached
Please find enclosed
I would like to apply
I am very interested in

# Generic closers
Thank you for your time and consideration
I look forward to hearing from you
I appreciate your consideration
At your earliest convenience

# Marketing-speak
dynamic environment
fast-paced environment
mission-driven
purpose-driven
cutting-edge
state-of-the-art
best-in-class

# Uncertain phrasing (replace with directness)
I believe I would be
I think I could be
I feel that I am
I might be a good fit
perfect fit
ideal candidate

# Self-deprecation / burden-framing (acknowledge gaps neutrally instead)
# Honest about a gap is good ("I'd ramp up on Go"); telling the employer
# the gap is a liability to them is the failure mode these catch.
a real cost
cost to the team
burden to the team
burden on the team
drain on the team
drain on resources
slow the team down
slow the squad down
hold the team back
liability to the team
a risk to the team
